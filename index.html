Ralph Bakshi

The Timeline of Ralph Bakshi

1938: Born on October 29 in Haifa, Palestine (now Israel) 1939: Family moves to Brownsville, Brooklyn, New York 1956: Begins working at Terrytoons animation studio as a cel polisher 1957: Promoted to animator at Terrytoons 1965: Becomes creative director at Terrytoons 1966: Moves to Paramount's animation division as creative director 1967: Creates "Mighty Heroes" series for Terrytoons 1972: Directs first feature film, "Fritz the Cat" - first animated film to receive an X rating 1973: Releases "Heavy Traffic", exploring themes of urban life and racism 1974: Directs "Coonskin", a controversial satire on race relations 1975: Begins work on "Hey Good Lookin'", but production is delayed 1977: Releases "Wizards", a post-apocalyptic fantasy film 1978: Directs animated "The Lord of the Rings", covering the first half of Tolkien's trilogy 1981: Releases "American Pop", chronicling four generations of a Russian Jewish immigrant family 1982: "Hey Good Lookin'" finally released after significant reworking 1983: Directs "Fire and Ice" in collaboration with famed fantasy artist Frank Frazetta 1987-1988: Creates and produces "Mighty Mouse: The New Adventures" TV series, launching careers of several animators 1989: Directs music video for "This Ain't the Summer of Love" by The Ramones 1992: Directs live-action/animation hybrid film "Cool World" starring Brad Pitt 1994: Develops but ultimately abandons "Coney Island of the Mind" project 1997: Creates "Spicy City", the first prime-time animated series aimed at adults on HBO 2003: Teaches animation at School of Visual Arts in New York 2015: Releases crowdfunded short film "Last Days of Coney Island" on Vimeo 2019: Announces development of "Wizards 2" but project remains unreleased.
